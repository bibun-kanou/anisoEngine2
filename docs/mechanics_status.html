Solvers

SolverStatusNotes
MPM (MLS-MPM) solid Affine particle-in-cell with SVD-based per-material constitutive branches. Supports ~80 material types. The G2P pass had a regression in the recent additions — fixed in commit da9bf86 after a GLSL reserved-word collision and SSBO cap overflow caused silent shader-link failure.
SPH solid Continuous surface force (CSF), thermal coupling, codimensional thin-film. Stable.
Eulerian Air solid MAC-grid Navier-Stokes + Boussinesq buoyancy + airtight cavity pressure + Gray-Scott reaction-diffusion automata. Multiple sub-solvers all working.
SDF Field solid Rigid kinematic geometry (boxes, segments). Materials carry magnetic mode/strength/dir, thermal coupling, friction. SDF objects are kinematic only — they don't feel forces back from particles (no two-way coupling).
Magnetic Poisson caveat Jacobi-relaxation Poisson solver, ~52-72 iterations per frame. Solves ∇²φ = -∇·M and produces H = -∇φ. Core math is correct. Caveats below in the magnetic section.
Electrostatic Poisson caveat Reuses the magnetic Jacobi + field shaders (Poisson is Poisson). Coulomb force F = qE applied in a dedicated post-MPM compute pass. Solver math is correct; behavior verified for ambient-E-driven ion streaming. Triboelectric contact charging is a stub.
XPBD constraints partial Distance-constraint Jacobi solver with int fixed-point accumulators. Rope material (80) wired up; press Y to spawn. Cloth bend constraints + graph-coloring for stiffness are TODO.

Magnetic family

Known issue: ferrofluid still tends to compact more than physical when an external source is present (e.g. holding M, scene magnet, ambient field). The latest commit (4450a86) made the cursor gate positional and reduced particle source_scale 24→10, which substantially reduced the runaway, but ferro near a strong source still over-clusters. Short term: tunable via Solver Strength sliders. Long term: needs proper demag-energy repulsion or per-cell M density saturation.

Mechanics inside the magnetic Kelvin-force path

MechanicStatusNotes
External-drive gate caveat Damps Kelvin force unless ambient_H, cursor (proximity-weighted), or a scene magnet is present. Cursor gate is positional as of 4450a86. Scene gate is still binary global — if a scene magnet exists anywhere, the gate is fully open everywhere. Result: ferro far from a magnet still gets self-feedback unclamped.
hf_suppress (sharpness damp) caveat Damps Kelvin force when the local field is much sharper than the bulk. Sharpness coefficient bumped 2.1 → 4.5 in 4450a86. Effective for individual-particle spikes; medium-sized clumps still slip through somewhat.
Kelvin body force caveat F = (χ/2) ∇|H|² applied to magnetic particles. Math is correct but ISOTROPIC — pulls particles toward any high-|H| region, including ones created by their own clustering (this is the dipole-dipole self-feedback issue).
Anisotropic chain force F = ∇(M·H) broken Implemented in c2b0f76, then disabled in da9bf86 when its SSBO pushed mpm_g2p past the driver cap. Will return as a dedicated post-MPM compute pass — same approach as Coulomb.
ferro_surface_force solid Capillary-style spike formation for fluid ferro materials. Active for FERRO_FLUID, HEAVY_FERRO, STICKY_FERRO. Long-tested.
Demag feedback (particle M rasterized into drive field) solid Rasterized on the final induction iteration so particles see each other's contribution self-consistently.
Persistent M_prev SSBO + relaxation solid Per-particle vec2 magnetization carried across frames with per-material relaxation rate. Enables hysteresis, Curie memory, Meissner thermal lag.
Ambient field (uniform background H) solid Earth-analog uniform contribution added in magnetic_field.comp. Drives ferrofluid to self-organize without any scene magnet. Works correctly.

Thermal coupling

Long-tested. SPH-MPM-Air-SDF cross-coupling all working. New magnetic-thermal couplings (Curie, Meissner, Eddy) are wired through and respond to heat sources.

Open issues & known limitations

  • Ferrofluid over-collapse with strong sources: clearly the biggest pain point. Cursor gate is positional but scene gate isn't; even with reduced source_scale, ferro near a strong magnet can compact more than physical. Best fixes from cheap to expensive: per-cell M density cap, demag-energy repulsion term, proper anisotropic dipole pair force as separate pass, multi-field Poisson solve (scene-only vs scene+particles).
  • Anisotropic chain force F = ∇(M·H): implemented and then removed when it pushed mpm_g2p over the SSBO cap. Will return as a dedicated post-MPM compute pass.
  • Triboelectric contact charging: stub. Material exists but charge stays zero. Need contact detection + transfer rules.
  • SDF rigid bodies feel no forces back: scene magnets and obstacles are kinematic-only. Two-way coupling (mass, velocity, torque) is in the roadmap.
  • Hard magnet remanence is a relaxation, not a hysteresis: persistent M decays toward Langevin target at 0.3/s. Real Preisach hysteresis would keep M flat below T_Curie even with no applied field.
  • Phase-field fracture has no Laplacian regularization: crack tips are slightly diffuse. AT2 PDE upgrade is a follow-up.
  • Showcase scenes are not scientifically validated: each scene description says "Expected: …", but we haven't checkpointed exactly which expected behaviors actually emerge versus need tuning. The mechanics-toggle UI is the right tool to do that — see "Roadmap" below.

Roadmap (priority order)

  1. Per-mechanic toggle panels — add to magnetic first, then electrostatic / MPM constitutive. Lets you isolate which sub-mechanic is producing wrong behavior. ~150-200 LOC per solver. This will dramatically improve our debugging cadence.
  2. Restore anisotropic chain force as a dedicated post-MPM pass.
  3. Per-cell M density cap in the particle rasterization to prevent runaway clumping.
  4. SDF spawn from creation menu — discoverability fix for scene magnets, soft iron blocks.
  5. Two-way SDF rigid-body dynamics — biggest single change remaining; magnets feel forces from particles.
  6. Scientific validation of showcase scenes — once toggle panels exist, walk through each scene with toggles to verify the documented "expected" behavior matches reality.
