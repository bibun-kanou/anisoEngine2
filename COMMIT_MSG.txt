mag: fix scene-magnet gate bypass + MAT 57 + add 3 new ferro variants

Critical fix (scene_gate):
The external-drive gate I added last time (8e95ded) gated the Kelvin force
on max(ambient_gate, cursor_gate) only. Scene SDF magnets produce clean
non-self gradients too, and a puddle of ferrofluid placed next to a
permanent magnet — with ambient=0 and no cursor held — got ext_drive=0
and no force at all. That broke one of the core ferro scenarios.

Extended gate to max(ambient_gate, cursor_gate, scene_gate) where
scene_gate is binary, tied to params_.enabled (Real Magnetics on). When
scene magnets are enabled, any gradient present is inherently external
in origin, so the force should pass through unconditionally. If Real
Magnetics is on but no actual magnets are placed, there are no gradients
anyway, so the bypass is harmless.

New uniform u_magnetic_scene_active in mpm_g2p.comp, set from
params_.enabled in mpm_solver.cpp.

MAT 57 MPM soft iron:
Susceptibility was 0.0018 — 200x smaller than MAT 58 rubber (0.34),
effectively dead as a Kelvin-responsive particle material. Raised to
0.22 (matching regular ferro), max_mag_force 1.2 -> 9.0. Now MPM soft
iron beads/shapes visibly pull toward scene magnets.

Three new magnetic fluid materials:

- DIAMAGNETIC_FLUID (68): negative susceptibility (-0.55). Particles
  pushed AWAY from field gradients. Pools at the EDGE of a magnet,
  hangs back from the cursor. Langevin M is inverted via a new
  polarity factor in magnetic_particles.comp. Pale blue color,
  medium-low density (1800).

- PARA_MIST (69): light aerosol. Low density (400), low susceptibility
  (0.08), low saturation. Drifts along field lines rather than clumping.
  Higher velocity cap + lower material cooling rate than ferrofluid.
  Silvery pale color.

- STICKY_FERRO (70): high-cohesion ferro. Strong magnetization
  (saturation 10.0, susc 0.55) combined with heavy velocity damping
  (0.35*dt). Instead of spikes it forms tight globules. Dark matte
  look. Good for 'magnetic blob' feel.

Wired into:
- mpm_g2p.comp: Kelvin force params + max_speed_cells + full_thermal +
  material_cooling + color defaults. abs() on susceptibility check so
  diamagnetic enters the force path.
